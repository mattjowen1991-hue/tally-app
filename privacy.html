Privacy Policy

Last updated: 9 May 2026

Tally ("we", "our", "the app") is a personal finance tracking application. Your privacy matters to us. This policy explains what data we collect, why, how we store it, and your rights.

The short version: Tally works fully offline. You don't need an account. If you do sign in, your data syncs to a secure cloud database so you can access it across devices. We never sell your data and never show ads. Anonymous usage analytics (which screens you open, which features you use - never your financial data) help us improve the app and can be turned off in Settings at any time.

1. What Data We Collect

Data you provide

  • Financial tracking data - bill names, amounts, due dates, categories, payment status; debt names, balances, interest rates, payment history; savings goal names, targets, deposits, withdrawals
  • Preferences - monthly income, currency, theme, notification settings, calculator settings
  • Account data (optional) - email address and authentication credentials if you choose to create an account for cloud sync

Data we collect automatically

  • Anonymous usage events (optional, can be turned off in Settings). When enabled, we collect a small set of structured events describing how you interact with the app - which screens you open, which buttons you tap, where you stop in a flow. These events are tied to a randomly-generated identifier stored in your device's secure storage, NOT your email or any personal information. The full list of events is published at docs/analytics-events.md and updated whenever it changes. See section 5 "Usage Analytics" below for details.
  • If you choose to send a bug report via Settings, diagnostic data about your device (model, OS version, screen size, battery level) is attached. This never includes your financial data, income, or bill/debt amounts.

Data we do NOT collect

  • Bank account numbers, sort codes, or card details
  • Location data
  • Contacts, photos, or files
  • Bill names, debt names, savings goal names, merchant names, or any free-text you type into the app (these never appear in usage analytics)
  • Amounts, balances, or any financial values (these never appear in usage analytics)
  • Advertising identifiers (IDFA, GAID, etc.)
  • Browsing history outside the app

2. Why We Collect It

  • Financial tracking data - to provide the core bill, debt, and savings tracking features you use the app for
  • Preferences - to remember your settings (currency, theme, notification time) between sessions
  • Account data - to authenticate you and sync your data across devices if you choose to sign in
  • Diagnostic data (only when you send a bug report) - to help us fix issues you report

3. How We Store Your Data

On your device (local storage)

All your financial data is stored locally on your device in a private database that only the Tally app can access. This works without an internet connection or account.

In the cloud (optional)

If you sign in, your data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and synced to our cloud database hosted by Supabase (PostgreSQL). Supabase provides encryption at rest for all stored data. Your data is accessible only to your authenticated account.

You are always in control. You can use Tally entirely offline with no account. Cloud sync is optional and can be disabled at any time by signing out.

4. Third-Party Services

We use a small number of third-party services, only for core functionality:

  • Supabase (EU region) - cloud database and authentication. Stores your synced financial data if you sign in.
  • Google Sign-In - optional authentication method (we receive your email address only).
  • PostHog (EU Cloud, Frankfurt) - anonymous usage analytics, only when enabled in Settings. Receives the events listed at docs/analytics-events.md. Never receives financial data or PII.

We do not use:

  • Google Analytics, Firebase Analytics, Meta Pixel, or any other tracker
  • Advertising networks or ad SDKs
  • Crash reporting services (Crashlytics, Sentry, etc.)
  • Social media tracking pixels

5. Usage Analytics

To understand how people use Tally, we collect a small set of anonymous usage events - for example, "user opened the import screen" or "user added a bill". This helps us decide what to build next and what to improve. We never collect your financial data, the names you give to bills, debts or goals, the merchants on your statements, any amounts, or any free-text input.

Where the data goes

Analytics events are processed by PostHog Inc. on PostHog's EU Cloud infrastructure (Frankfurt, Germany). Data is stored in the EU and is not transferred to the United States. Our lawful basis under UK GDPR is consent, which you give when you tap "Got it" on the analytics card on second launch, or when you leave the toggle on in Settings.

Identification

Events are tied to a random UUID generated on first launch and stored in your device's secure keystore (iOS Keychain / Android EncryptedSharedPreferences). This is not your email, your name, or any device identifier. If you sign in, the random UUID is linked to your Tally account ID for retention analysis - we still do not use your email or other personal information as an identifier.

Your controls

  • Turn analytics on or off at any time: Settings → Privacy → Share usage analytics
  • Request deletion of all your analytics data: email mattjowen1991@gmail.com from the address linked to your Tally account
  • Retention: events are kept for 12 months rolling, then deleted automatically
  • Full event list: docs/analytics-events.md - we update this whenever we add, remove, or change an event

Closed beta default: ON, with a one-time disclosure card on second launch. Public v1 launch default: OFF. The toggle is always available in Settings either way.

6. Data Sharing

We never sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We do not share your financial information with advertisers, data brokers, or any third party for marketing purposes.

Your data is shared only with:

  • Supabase - our cloud database provider, solely for the purpose of storing and syncing your data
  • Google - solely for authentication if you use Google Sign-In

7. Data Retention

  • Local data - stored on your device until you delete it via Settings or uninstall the app
  • Cloud data - stored as long as your account exists. You can delete all cloud data at any time by signing out and using the "Clear All Data" option in Settings
  • Authentication data - managed by Supabase. Session tokens expire and are refreshed automatically

8. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, EU GDPR, and applicable data protection laws, you have the right to:

  • Access - view all your data within the app at any time
  • Rectification - edit any of your data through the app's edit screens
  • Erasure - delete all local data via Settings > Clear All Data. Cloud data can be removed by contacting us
  • Portability - your data is stored in standard formats and can be accessed through the app
  • Restriction - you can use the app entirely offline without sharing any data
  • Withdraw consent - sign out at any time to stop cloud sync

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at tallytracking@gmail.com.

9. Security

  • All data transmitted between your device and our servers is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS
  • Cloud data is encrypted at rest by our database provider (Supabase)
  • Passwords are hashed using industry-standard bcrypt (never stored in plaintext)
  • Local data is stored in Android's private app sandbox, inaccessible to other apps
  • Authentication uses secure JWT tokens with automatic expiry and refresh

10. Android Permissions

Tally requests only the minimum permissions needed:

  • INTERNET Cloud sync and authentication
  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS Bill and debt payment reminders (requested at runtime, optional)
  • VIBRATE Haptic feedback on button presses
  • RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED Reschedule notification alarms after device restart

We do not request access to your camera, contacts, location, microphone, calendar, SMS, or files.

11. Children's Privacy

Tally is designed for adults managing their personal finances. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 16. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated through the app or via email if you have an account. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.
